The Bag

Poem by INI



An idle walk to the corner store,

In search of something to smoothen out the edge of the day,

Or maybe open a a taxing lock on your want for more,

To let free the air that will lead the way,

After the satisfaction of the pour,

Comes steps with a handful, to take your mind astray,

More than needed is the price of the tour,





To keep the high looks, you head to the counter,

To exchange valuables with the clerk you encounter,

The sounds of the increasing wage causes a grin,

And a realization it wasn't as important as it seemed,





The clerk grabs a bag made of one atom weight of a dozen,

And another one away from nothing,

For every one of the heavier is two of the other,

With the former having a double bond together,

Between itself, to make the chain come in order,

The polyethylene taking the form of a trigonal planar,





The nonpolar molecule is trust down a tube for melting,

Made smooth on the other end with thin sharp edges so exalting,

Then it rolls on a belt getting cut into the desired shape,

And shipped to the destination in rolls of tape,





The bag sizzles as the items rush for the bottom causing an impact,

The tension is eased by the elasticity to keep it intact,

As it changes hands it is welcomed with a firm grip,

And a smile to ensure Elation of what is theirs to keep,

Firm steps are taken radiating the pride of ownership,





Not to fast, and not too slow, just enough to get to the door,

Completing another cycle of a weekly trip to the corner store.
